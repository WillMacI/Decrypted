Incident Summary

On the evening of March 22, 1974, the Toronto Fire Department responded to a 911 call at approximately 8:45 PM reporting a fire at 47 Digital Drive, the residence of Mr. Mers Kutt. Upon extinguishing the fire, investigators determined the cause to be suspicious and potentially indicative of arson. Mr. Kutt could not be located at the scene, and there has been no communication from him since.

Investigation Details

  • Incident Location: The residence of Mers Kutt, located at 47 Digital Drive, North York, Toronto, showed signs of forced entry on the rear door, suggesting unauthorized access prior to the fire.
  • Evidence Collected:
    • Samples of an accelerant detected at multiple points around the property.
    • A broken lock and damaged door frame on the rear entrance.
    • Several technological devices and components, some of which belonged to the early development stages of the MCM/70 computer, were salvaged for further examination.
  • Witness Statements:
    • Neighbors reported seeing an unfamiliar vehicle parked near the property earlier in the day.
    • Sounds of disturbance were heard from the property around 8:00 PM, shortly before Mr. Kutt was last seen.

Action Taken

  • A missing person report has been filed for Mers Kutt.
  • The arson investigation unit is analyzing the evidence collected from the scene to identify the accelerant and possible sources.
  • The local and surrounding area hospitals have been alerted.
  • A BOLO (Be On the Look Out) has been issued for Mr. Kutt across the province.

Leads

  • The unfamiliar vehicle's description has been circulated to patrol units for identification and location.
  • The technological devices recovered from the fire are being examined for any data that might point to potential threats or motives related to Mr. Kutt's disappearance.
